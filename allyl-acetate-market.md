<p><p>The Allyl Acetate market comprises primarily two production methods: the Allyl Acetate Hydrolysis Method and the Propylene Oxide (PO) Isomerization Method. The hydrolysis method involves the reaction of allyl alcohol with acetic acid, producing allyl acetate through a reversible reaction. In contrast, the PO isomerization method utilizes propylene oxide to yield allyl acetate via a catalytic process. These methods differ in their efficiency, cost, and environmental impact, influencing their adoption in industrial applications.</p></p>
