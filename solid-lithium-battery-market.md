<p><p>The solid lithium battery market encompasses two primary types: polymer-based solid-state lithium batteries and solid-state lithium batteries with inorganic solid electrolytes. Polymer-based batteries use flexible, lightweight polymer materials as electrolytes, offering advantages like enhanced safety and design versatility. In contrast, batteries with inorganic solid electrolytes leverage materials such as ceramics, providing higher ionic conductivity and stability at elevated temperatures. Both types aim to improve energy density, safety, and lifespan compared to conventional lithium-ion batteries, catering to various applications.</p></p>
