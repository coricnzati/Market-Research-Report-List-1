<p><p>Orthopaedic imaging equipment encompasses various technologies used for diagnosing musculoskeletal conditions. X-ray systems provide quick imaging of bones and joints, while CT scanners offer detailed cross-sectional views for complex fractures. MRI systems deliver high-resolution images of soft tissues, essential for assessing injuries. EOS imaging systems specialize in low-dose, full-body imaging in an upright position, aiding in spinal assessments. Ultrasound is used for real-time imaging of soft tissues and joints, while nuclear imaging systems visualize bone metabolism and function, assisting in treatment planning.</p></p>
