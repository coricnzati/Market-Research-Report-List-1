<p><p>The ophthalmic sutures market is divided into two main types: natural and synthetic sutures. Natural sutures are derived from biological sources, such as collagen or silk, offering biocompatibility but may have drawbacks like greater variability and potential for infection. On the other hand, synthetic sutures are made from man-made materials like nylon or polypropylene, providing consistent quality and a range of properties such as absorbability. Each type has distinct advantages, catering to different surgical needs and patient requirements in ophthalmic procedures.</p></p>
