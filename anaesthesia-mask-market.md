<p><p>The anaesthesia mask market primarily includes facial masks and nasal masks, each serving distinct purposes. Facial masks cover the entire face, providing effective ventilation during procedural sedations and general anaesthesia. They ensure a snug fit for optimal gas delivery. In contrast, nasal masks deliver anaesthetic gases through the nostrils, allowing for more targeted ventilation and often greater patient comfort. Both types are essential in medical settings, catering to varying patient needs and procedural requirements, ensuring safety and efficacy in anaesthesia practices.</p></p>
