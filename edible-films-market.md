<p><p>The edible films market consists of various types, including protein, polysaccharides, lipids, and others. Protein-based films, derived from sources like gelatin and casein, provide good mechanical properties and barrier functionality. Polysaccharide films, made from materials like starch and alginate, are known for their biodegradability and film-forming ability. Lipid-based films offer moisture resistance and are often used in food packaging to enhance shelf life. Other types may include blends or innovative materials that cater to specific applications in food preservation and safety.</p></p>
