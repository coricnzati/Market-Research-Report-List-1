<p><p>Patient recruitment for clinical trials varies across four phases. Phase I focuses on safety and involves a small group of healthy volunteers. Phase II assesses efficacy and side effects in a larger patient population with the targeted condition. Phase III confirms effectiveness and monitors adverse reactions, often involving thousands of participants for regulatory approval. Phase IV, or post-marketing studies, evaluates long-term effects and broader use in the general population, refining information on risks and benefits of a drug or treatment.</p></p>
