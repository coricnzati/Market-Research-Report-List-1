<p><p>Antenatal screening encompasses various diagnostic methods to assess fetal health during pregnancy. Amniocentesis involves extracting amniotic fluid to test for genetic conditions. Chorionic Villus Sampling (CVS) collects placental tissue to identify chromosomal abnormalities. Ultrasonic testing, commonly known as ultrasound, uses sound waves to visualize the fetus and assess its development and well-being. Other screening methods may include blood tests and non-invasive prenatal testing (NIPT), which provide additional insights into genetic risks without invasive procedures, enhancing overall prenatal care.</p></p>
